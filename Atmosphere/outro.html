What You've Learned:
  • Pressure is defined as force per unit area (P = F/A), measured in Pascals (Pa = N/m²)
  • Atmospheric pressure is the weight per unit area of the air column above any point, equal to 101,325 Pa at sea level
  • Torricelli invented the mercury barometer in 1643, discovering that air has weight and exhibits pressure
  • Modern barometers (aneroid, electronic) measure pressure without using mercury
  • Metric (SI) pressure units: Pa, Atm, bar, millibar (mbar), and hectopascal (hPa)
  • Practical units: psi (tire pressure), mm Hg/Torr (medicine), in Hg (aviation, US weather)
