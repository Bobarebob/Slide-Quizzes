What Is Pressure?

Defining Pressure and Atmospheric Pressure

Pressure (General Definition)
Pressure on a surface is defined as the force applied perpendicularly to that surface, divided by the area of that surface. Pressure is a scalar quantity.
P = F / A
Pressure = Force ÷ Area  |  Metric (SI) unit: 1 Pa = 1 N / 1 m²

The key insight is that pressure depends not just on the amount of force applied, but also on the area over which it acts. A small force concentrated on a tiny area can produce an enormous pressure — this is why a sharp needle can pierce skin while a broad palm cannot.

In metric (SI) units, pressure is measured in Pascals (Pa). One Pascal equals one Newton of force applied over one square meter of area.

Atmospheric Pressure (Definition)
Atmospheric pressure is the weight of the column of air directly above a particular altitude, divided by the cross-sectional area of that column of air.

At sea level, we are at the bottom of an enormous ocean of air that extends hundreds of kilometers above us. The weight of all that air pressing down creates atmospheric pressure. The higher you go, the less air is above you — and the lower the atmospheric pressure.

💡 Did you know? At sea level, air pressure is 101,325 Pascal (Pa), which is also equal to 14.7 pounds per square inch (psi). This means the atmosphere pushes down on every square inch of your body with 14.7 pounds of force — yet you feel nothing, because the air pressure inside your body (specifically your lungs) pushes outward with equal force!
