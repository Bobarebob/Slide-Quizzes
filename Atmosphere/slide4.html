Pressure Units — Part 2

PSI, Millimeters of Mercury (Torr) & Inches of Mercury

Context: The three units on this slide — psi, mm Hg (Torr), and in Hg — are historically significant and still used extensively in medicine, aviation, industry, and meteorology, particularly in the United States. All three can be directly converted to and from 1 Atm.
Pounds per Square Inch
psi
1 Atm = 14.696 psi

A traditional US unit: the force of one pound-force applied over one square inch of area. Widely used in the United States for:

  • → Tire pressure (typical car: 30–35 psi)
  • → Scuba / diving tank pressure
  • → Industrial hydraulics and pneumatics
Millimeters of Mercury
mm Hg (Torr)
1 Atm = 760 mm Hg = 760 Torr

Derived directly from Torricelli's barometer: the pressure that supports a 1 mm column of mercury. The Torr (named for Torricelli) was defined to be exactly 1/760 of a standard atmosphere — effectively numerically identical to mm Hg. Key uses:

  • → Blood pressure (systolic/diastolic in mm Hg)
  • → Vacuum technology and physics labs
  • → Historical meteorology records
Inches of Mercury
in Hg
1 Atm = 29.921 in Hg

The US equivalent of mm Hg: the pressure that supports a 1 inch tall mercury column. Standard for US aviation altimeter setting. Key uses:

  • → Aviation altimeters
  • → US weather reports and TV forecasts
  • → Historical barometer readings in English-speaking countries

💡 Key relationship: 1 Pa = 1 N / 1 m²
All units are linear — to convert from Atm to any unit, simply multiply:
e.g., 0.5 Atm = 7.35 psi = 380 mm Hg = 14.96 in Hg = 506.6 hPa
💡 Remember: 1 hPa = 1 mbar (numerically identical — see Slide 3). And 760 mm Hg = 760 Torr (also identical).
