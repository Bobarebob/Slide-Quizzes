Lab 4A — Diffraction

Measuring the Wavelength of Light

Welcome to Lab 4A — Diffraction. In this exercise you will use a diffraction grating to split laser light into its component colors, then measure the resulting interference pattern to calculate the wavelength of each laser. This is the same fundamental technique used in real-world spectroscopy — the tool astronomers use to decode the chemical composition, temperature, and motion of stars.

📌 Learning Objectives
  • Understand how a diffraction grating separates light by wavelength
  • Measure grating-to-wall distance L and dot separation D for three laser colors
  • Apply the grating equation: λ = d × sin(θ) to find each wavelength
  • Calculate percent error by comparing your result to the accepted value
  • Use a guided RPN-style calculator to practice the step-by-step calculation process
