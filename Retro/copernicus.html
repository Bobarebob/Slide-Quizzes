Nicolaus Copernicus

The Man Who Moved the Earth

Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543) was a Polish mathematician and astronomer who formulated a model of the Solar System (and by extension the Universe) that placed the Sun rather than Earth at its center. This heliocentric model revolutionized our understanding of the cosmos and laid the foundation for modern astronomy.

The Challenge of Retrograde Motion

Copernicus's Elegant Solution

Copernicus realized that retrograde motion was an illusion created by Earth's own motion around the Sun. When Earth, moving on an inner orbit, overtakes a slower outer planet like Mars, that planet appears to move backward. Similarly, when a faster inner planet like Venus laps Earth, it too exhibits retrograde motion from our perspective.

This simple geometric explanation eliminated the need for epicycles and demonstrated the power of the heliocentric model. It was a triumph of Occam's Principle‚Äîthe simpler explanation proved to be the correct one.
